1920 - 1929

Women's Suffrage (1920)

The 19th Amendment was ratified on August 19, 1920, allowing women to vote in elections.

Chinese Communists Founding (1921)

The founding of the Chinese Communist Party by Chen Duxiu and Li Dazhao on July 23, 1921.

The Irish Civil War Starts (1922)

The Irish Civil War starts on June 28, 1922 because of two opposing groups, the pro-treaty Provisional Government and the anti-treaty Irish Republican Army (IRA), fighting over the Anglo-Irish Treaty.

Great Kanto Earthquake (1923)

The Great Kanto Earthquake is known as one of the worst natural disatars that hit mainland Japan, killing around 150,000 people.

Transition From Lenin To Stalin (1924)

Valdmair Lenin died on January 21, 1924 and his successor was Joseph Stalin would take leadership of the Soviet Union. Stalin would run the Soviet Union for almost 30 years.

The Great Gatsby (1925)

On April 10, 1925, F. Scott Fitzgerald would publish on of the most famous books, The Great Gatsby.

Winnie the Pooh (1926)

On October 14, 1926, A. A. Milne would publish Winnie the Pooh.

The Royal and Parliamentary Titles Act of 1927 (1927)

It renamed the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland to the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. It acknowledging that the Irish Free State was no longer part of the UK.

First Woman to Cross the Alantic (1928)

Aviator Amelia Earhart became the first woman to make a transatlantic flight, although she did make this flight as a passenger, not as a pilot.

The Birth of Martin Luther King Jr. (1929)

On January 15, 1929, Martin Luther King Jr was born.

1930 - 1939

The Salt March (1930)

An act of civil disobedience led by Mohandas Gandhi to protest British rule in India

1931 Yangtze–Huai River Floods (1931)

The Yangtze River floods caused the deaths of 3. 7 million people directly and indirectly. Poor river management caused the banks to flood.

Amelia Earhart's Flight Across the Alantic Solo (1932)

In May 1932 Earhart became the first woman to fly solo across in the Atlantic.

Reich Chancellor of Germany (1933)

Adolf Hitler was appointed as Chancellor of Germany by President Hindenburg.

Night of the Long Knives (1934)

A purging of Nazi leaders ordered by Hitler. Hitler feared that his paramilitary SA had become too powerful, so he ordered his SS guards to murder the organizations leaders.

Flying from Hawaii to California (1935)

Amelia Earhart becomes the first person to fly solo from Hawaii to California

King Edward VIII (1936)

On January 20, 1936, King George V died and his son Edward succeded him. Edward became King Edward VIII.

Second Sino-Japanese War Begans (1937)

Conflict broke out between China and Japan because of Chinese resistence against Japan's influence and territorial expansion.

Kristallnacht (1938)

Kristallnacht or the Night of Broken Glass was a pogrom against Jews carried out by the Nazi Party's Sturmabteilung paramilitary forces along with civilians throughout Nazi Germany on 9–10 November 1938. The German authorities did not intervene with what was happening.

World War 2 Begins (1939)

The reason of World War 2 was Hitler invading Poland causing the UK and France to join the war.

1940 - 1949

Taking Paris (1940)

German forces took Paris on June 14. The Germans imposed a curfew on Parisians for 8 p.m. that same evening. By the time the German tanks entered the French capital, a large population had fled the city.

Pearl Habor (1941)

A surprise attack by the Japanese against the United States. Japan attacked a naval base in Hawaii. This would lead to United States to enter World War 2.

White Christmas (1942)

White Christmas by Bing Crosby was released and became a major hit in Europe and America.

Battle Of Stalingrad (1943)

A major battle on the Eastern Front of World War II where Nazi Germany and its allies lost due to the weather conditions.

D-Day (1944)

On June 6, 1944 more than 160,000 allied forces landed in Nazi occupied France. It was the biggest air, land, and sea invasion.

End Of World War 2 (1945)

On September 2, 1945, Japan surrendered, signifying the end of World War 2.

Project Diana (1946)

This was the first experiment in radar astronomy and the first active attempt to probe another celestial body by the United States.

Cold War Begins (1947)

The beginning of the Cold War happened on March 12, 1947. It was geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union and their respective allies, the Western Bloc and the Eastern Bloc, which began following World War II.

Marshall Plan (1948)

The Marshall Plan was an American initiative enacted in 1948 to provide foreign aid to Western Europe. The United States transfer billions of dollars to help Western Europe.

People's Republic of China (1949)

The Communists gained control of mainland China and established the People's Republic of China (PRC) in 1949, forcing the leadership of the Republic of China to retreat to the island of Taiwan.

1950 - 1959

Beginning of the Korean War (1950)

A civil war between North Korea and South Korea. Many other countries participated in the war by supporting one side of the war.

22nd Amendment (1951)

Congress passed the 22nd Amendment limiting the amount of times a president can serve to two terms.

Queen Elizabeth II (1952)

King George VI died on Feburary 6, 1952. His daugher Princess Elizabth, the Duchess of Edinburgh, became Queen Elizabeth II.

End of the Korean War (1953)

North Korea, South Korea, the United States, and the People's Republic of China agreed to an armistice that ended the war.

Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka (1954)

Supreme Court ruled unanimously that racial segregation of children in public schools was unconstitutional.

Warsaw Pact (1955)

This was a mutual defense treaty among the Soviet countries and Soviet satellite nations as an equivalent to the Western powers’ formation of NATO.

Heartbreak Hotel (1956)

Elvis entered US music charts for the first time this year, with his recording of “Heartbreak Hotel.”

Sputnik 2 (1957)

The Soviets Sputik 2 was the first mission to launch an animal to Earth's orbit, a dog, called Laika.

Explorer 1 (1958)

The United States successfully launched Explorer 1 into Earth’s orbit. It was the first successful satellite launch for the United States.

Alaska and Hawaii (1959)

Alaska and Hawaii both became the 49th and 50th states of the United States respectively.

1960 - 1969

Great Chilean Earthquake (1960)

It is the most powerful earthquake ever recorded. 9.4–9.6 on the magnitude scale.

JFK Elected (1961)

JFK became the youngest candidate to become president.

The Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)

During the Cuban Missile Crisis, leaders of the U.S. and the Soviet Union engaged in a tense, 13-day political and military standoff in October 1962 over the installation of nuclear-armed Soviet missiles on Cuba.

JFK Assassination (1963)

John F. Kennedy, the 35th president of the United States, was assassinated on Friday, November 22, 1963, at 12:30 p.m. CST in Dallas, Texas, while riding in a presidential motorcade through Dealey Plaza.

Civil Rights Act of 1964 (1964)

President Lyndon Johnson signs the Civil Rights Act of 1964, officially abolishing racial segregation in the country.

Civil Rights Act of 1965 (1965)

It outlawed the discriminatory voting practices adopted in many southern states after the Civil War, including literacy tests as a prerequisite to voting.

Space Race US vs Soviet Union (1966)

The Soviet’s Luna 9 gave the world the first unmanned, controlled, rocket-assisted Moon landing. The United States responded with NASA’s Gemini 8 accomplishing the first docking in space. The Soviets came back with Luna 10, which was the first space probe to enter orbit around the Moon.

Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967)

1967 is the year The Beatles released their famous album, Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band.

Martin Luther King Jr. Assassination (1968)

Martin Luther King, a major civil rights leader in the United States, was murdered at The Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee on April 4th.

Apollo 11 (1969)

U.S. astronauts became the first people to walk on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and lunar module pilot Edwin (“Buzz”) Aldrin, Jr., on July 20, 1969, became the first people to land on the Moon and walk the lunar surface.

1970 - 1979

Apollo 13 (1970)

On April 11, Apollo 13 was launched. Apollo 13's oxygen tank exploded causing the mission to change to getting the astronauts home safe. On April 17, 1970, Apollo 13 returned to Earth.

Soyuz 11 (1971)

The Soviet launched three of their cosmonauts; Vladislav Volkov, Viktor Patsayev, and Georgi Dobrovolsky into space aboard the Soyuz 11 on June 30.

Pong (1972)

In 1972, the first every video game was released, Pong’s arcade version by Atari.

Endangered Species Act (1973)

Nixon signed the Endangered Species Act into law, protecting animals that are endangered.

Richard Nixon Resignation (1974)

President Richard Nixon announced his resignation following the Watergate Scandal on August 8.

Vietnam War Ends (1975)

After years of brutal fighting, Saigon fell to the Northern forces. American personnel and many civilians from South Vietnam evacuated the city. With the fall of Saigon and the unconditional surrender of the South, the war in Vietnam came to a close.

NASA’s Viking 2 (1976)

NASA’s Viking 2 landed on Mars. Shortly after landing, Viking 2 got to work, sending the first-ever close-up, color photos of the Mars surface.

Star Wars Released (1977)

The first movie of the Star Wars franchise, Star Wars: Episode IV – A New Hope released on May 25, 1977.

Test Tube Baby (1978)

The first test tube baby was born in the United Kingdom. The impact of this reroductive manipulating the natural world and would affect millions of lives around the world.

First Female Prime Minister of the UK (1979)

Margaret Thatcher became the first female prime minister.

On Feburary 11th, 1990, Nelson Mandela walked free out of prison. He served his first 18 years of his 27 years in prison. F.W. de Klerk helped free Mandela.

1991 is the day where the World Wide Web was released to the public and held information that can be accesible from any computer.

Presidentt of South Africa (1994)

Former American football player OJ Simpson was accused of murdering his ex-wife, Nicole Brown-Simpson and Ron Goldman, a friend of Nicole’s. It was called the “trial of the century” and truly became a cultural phenomenon. It was very highly publicized and there was a great deal of speculation regarding the guilt or innocence of Mr. Simpson, especially after the crimes he later committed after a verdict of “not guilty” at this trial.

Nintendo 64 (1996)

1996 was the year that the Nintendo 64, or N64, was released. Named because of its 64-bit central processing unit, this gaming system represented another step in game console development and gave the world several memorable games, such as Super Mario 64, Mario Kart 64, GoldenEye 007, and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.

2000- 2009

Mission Impossible II (2000)

Mission Impossible II was the highest grossing film at the box office.

9/11 (2001)

On a bright morning of September 11 a Tuesday, an American Airlines Boeing 767 full of 20,000 gallons of jet fuel flew into the World Trade Center's Northern Tower in New York City. President Bush on October 7 announced that the USA was going to war in Afghanistan and on the 26 of October signed the Patriot Act, an anti-terrorism law, following the September 11 attacks on the Pentagon and the world trade center.

The Sri Lankan Government and Tamil Tigers (2002)

At The Hague, the trial for Slobodan Milosevic, former Yugoslav’s leader, was opened with charges of crimes committed against humanity. Moreover, a 19-year-old civil war ended after the Sri Lankan government and Tamil Tigers signed a cease-fire agreement.

SARS Epidemic (2003)

The World Health Organization declared SARS a threat to global health on March 12. In the eight months later, SARS had killed roughly 775 in 29 countries, putting a threat to globalization regarding public health. On July 5, WHO announced that, the transmission of SARS from person to person had ceased.

First Legal Same-Sex Marriage (2004)

On May 17, Marcia Kadish 56 married Tanya McCloskey 52, in the first ever and legal same-sex marriage in Cambridge Hall Massachusetts. On that day, over 77 same-sex couples got married while hundreds of other same-sex couples requested for marriage certificates.

7/7 Bombings Of London (2005)

In July of 2005, the infamous 7/7 bombings of London took place. On this day, four Islamic extremists detonated bombs in central London, targeting civilians on the London Underground and on a bus as well. Over 50 people were killed and several hundred were wounded.

United States and Other Allied Forces Invaded Iraq (2006)

After the United States and other allied forces invaded Iraq in 2003, Sadaam Hussein, the longtime dictator of Iraq, had been overthrown. In 2006, Hussein was sentenced to die and his execution was carried out.

First Generation iPhone (2007)

This was the year that Steve Jobs, CEO of Apple Inc., announced the first generation iPhone. This was one of the first-ever “Smartphone” and was highly acclaimed, especially for its innovative use of a large touchscreen as the main means of interacting with the device.

Falcon 1 (2008)

This was the year that the Falcon 1 spacecraft of SpaceX was successfully launched into Earth’s orbit. It was the first privately developed spacecraft to do so. As space exploration through government agencies such as NASA began to wane, SpaceX offered the world a glimpse at a possible new future of space exploration.

Barack Obama (2009)

Barack Obama became the first African-American to become president.

2010- 2019

2010 Haiti Earthquake (2010)

On 12 January 2010, a 7.0 magnitude earthquake struck Haiti, leaving its capital Port-au-Prince devastated. About 220,000 people were reportedly killed, among them, 102 United Nations staff who lost their lives when the building housing the mission there, known as MINUSTAH, collapsed.

The Tohoku Earthquake (2011)

On March 11, 2011, Japan experienced the strongest earthquake in its recorded history. The earthquake struck below the North Pacific Ocean, 130 kilometers (81 miles) east of Sendai, the largest city in the Tohoku region, a northern part of the island of Honshu. The Tohoku earthquake caused a tsunami.

Russian Presidential Elections (2012)

In Russia, Vladimir Putin won the presidential elections with a landslide vote of 64% giving him his third term in office.

Frozen (2013)

Frozen, the animation film was the highest grossing film of 2013.

Arresting El Chapo (2014)

Eight days after Valentine’s Day, a joint task force made up of law enforcement from the USA and Mexico arrested Joaquin Guzman Loera, infamously known as El Chapo, the leader of the Sinaloa Cartel. The apprehending of El Chapo was at an apartment building in Mazatlán, Mexico after evading arrest for more than a decade. In 2001, El Chapo broke out of a Mexican prison where he was serving a 20-year sentence for drug trafficking, conspiracy, and bribery. Before his arrest, songs glorified his ability to elude arrest for more than a decade. His criminal activities perpetrated by his foot soldiers are responsible for the deaths and destruction of millions of lives worldwide through drug addiction, corruption, and violence.

Star Wars: The Force Awakens (2015)

Star Wars: The Force Awakens was the highest grossing film of the year with over $2 billion in ticket sales around the world.

Donald Trump (2016)

On November 8th Donald Trump was elected as the new President of the United States of America.

First Solar Eclipse Since 1918 (2017)

2017 is the first total solar eclipse happens again.

Formal End of the Korean War (2018)

A historic summit between North and South Korea on April 27th led to a formal end of the Korean War, as the two countries unitedly welcomed the rest of the world to the 2018 Winter Olympics held in Pyeongchang.

Climate Change (2019)

It was the year when the concentrations of carbon dioxide hit the highest mark, thus locking in climate change.

2020- 2022

COVID-19 Begins (2020)

2020 marks the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic. COVID-19 infection rates rose forcing the world to shut down.

Joe Biden (2021)

Biden was inaugurated as the 46th president of the US on January 6th, 2021. Kamala Harris became the US first female, Black and South Asian vice president. After taking office, Biden spoke at a ceremony to mark 500,000 US deaths from COVID-19.

2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine War (2022)

On 24 February 2022, Russia invaded Ukraine in a major escalation of the Russo-Ukrainian War that began in 2014. The invasion caused Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II, with more than 9.5 million Ukrainians fleeing the country and a third of the population displaced.
